Product Development

Objective

The objective of an engagement is to conduct a comprehensive review and to identify opportunities to enhance the R&D activities of the Client to be more effective and, for a young company, to mature the R&D methods and processes. This involves assessing the current product development planning and pipeline, direction and content, and suggesting an appropriate improvement plan.

We will conduct a thorough analysis and investigation of the current structure and content of the Client’s R&D efforts. We will offer analyses and recommendations based on the review on how to improve and possibly better align R&D efforts with business strategy and market conditions. Quality and Reliability will be given special attention. Methods like Product Development Portfolio management, Phase Gate processes, Risk Analyses and Mitigation Assessments, Business case analyses of every R&D effort and other methods will be evaluated if useful to the Client. The overall technology roadmap will be reviewed (if not existing it will be drafted) and looked at for improvements.  We will also provide general training and coaching to the R&D team.



Phase 1: Initial Report

We will be visiting the Client’s locations as needed and carry out these specific activities:

  • Conduct interviews (as applicable) with investors, board members, executives and managers, key R&D personnel and if possible with some selected customers and key suppliers
  • Review current activities, documents and reports of the R&D efforts, product management and manufacturing functions
  • Assess the effectiveness of the current R&D organization, resource allocation and incentive system
  • Review in detail the short and medium term R&D plans of the Client, including issues such as prioritization and resource allocation for new product development, cost reduction opportunities, quality and reliability improvements, etc.
  • Report on progress and findings to the Company at the end of the first round of interviews and reviews and initiate a detailed discussion of these findings with the Client. This report will be given verbally and supported with a written summary of key findings
  • The goal after this discussion is to develop and agree with the Client, specifically the R&D management, on an improvement plan or revised R&D plan, with clear deliverables and performance benchmarks.
  • Identify technology areas of strengths and areas with opportunities to improve through reviews and e.g.  organize or attend design workshops on specific ongoing projects, or technology areas currently of highest interest to the Client.
  • Issue and present to the Client a draft written report at end of the this planning engagement

Following the issuance of the draft Phase 1 report, we will engage in further reviews of findings with the Client and we will update the draft report as necessary. This Phase 1 typically takes about two working weeks.  The final Phase 1 report will be typically issued within 2 weeks following the final review session.



Phase 2: Implementation and ongoing Monitoring

During implementation of the improvement plan resulting from the Phase 1 report, we will visit the Client typically once a month for a period of one to two weeks.  During such follow up visits, we will also provide general coaching and training to the R&D team.  The frequency of such visits can be reduced to typically once every two months or to another frequency as is agreed once the Client is satisfied that the improvement plan has gained sufficient traction.

In between visits we will as necessary also attend video or telephone conference calls to guide and monitor the implementation.

Management

Currently Dr. Esser is the CEO of Albert Esser Consulting, Inc. since 2010, offering a wide variety of services in the technology sector with clients national and international. Dr. Esser started his Consulting career with Albert Esser LLC, later acquired by Econdius, LLC. Dr. Esser was the Head of Electrical Systems at Joby Aero, from 2016 to 2017, a leading eVTOL developer, responsible for all electrical Systems on the airplane. Prior he was COO at 21.co, from 2013 - 2015, a leading crypto compute company, responsible for procurement and operations of all products, equipment and services, and for all data center and mechanical systems designs. Dr. Esser was with fortu AG and held the positions CEO, COO and prior VP Engineering, respectively between 2011 to 2012.

Prior Dr. Esser was Vice President Power and Infrastructure Solutions, Dell Inc. until 2009 where he was responsible for Dell’s Infrastructure Product line and Dell’s global Power and Cooling Initiative, enhancing Dell’s enterprise class IT solutions, providing insight gained from customers to the Server, Storage, Data Center Solutions and Services product leaders to maximize the value of the Dell solutions.

Prior to Eaton, Dr. Esser was the Chief Technology Officer and Vice President, Emerson Network Power, Emerson Electric. He served as Technology Business Leader, Network Power Group, on the Emerson Technology Council. As CTO, he was overseeing the global R&D and product development of Emerson Network Power, which has R&D locations in 6 continents. The product lines included UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply), Precision Air-conditioning, Telecom-DC Systems, Monitoring Systems and others. Concurrently Dr. Esser was also the VP Global Design DC-Systems and had direct global responsibility for the global R&D and Engineering of the Telecom DC-Systems Business.

During his Emerson Network Power tenure Dr. Esser was selected as a Founding Member and currently Member Emeritus of the GridWise Architecture Council (GWAC), sponsored by the DOE, a team of industry leaders who are shaping the guiding principles, or architecture, of a highly intelligent and interactive electric system, one ripe with decision-making information exchange and market-based opportunities.

Previously Dr. Esser held the position Senior Vice President Corporate Research at Hilti, Liechtenstein. The fields of Research he was responsible for included Work Science, Advanced Material Science and Analyses, Advanced Electronics and Systems, New Business Idea finding including business case studies, Engineering Services and others. He had global responsibility for joint research with Universities, Government and strategic partners.

Dr. Esser’s career with Emerson started as the Corporate Director Technology. In this assignment he was administering Emerson’s Strategic Investment Program aimed at highly visible technology based growth opportunities.

In Dr. Esser’s early career he held several positions with increasing responsibilities in GE. He started as Project Manager in GE’s Corporate R&D Center, Power Controls Program, and became Manager of this group. Dr. Esser had responsibility for GE’s Research in the field of Electric Motors, Drives, Power Electronics, and Advanced Controls for these fields. Projects included Hybrid Bus (for NYC), Locomotive Traction Drive, High Speed Starter/Generator, high power inverters and others. Dr. Esser then was appointed as Manager Imaging Subsystems, and later Global Manager at GE Medical Systems, MRI. He was one of GE’s first Certified Master Blackbelt and was part of the initial 6 Sigma effort in GE Medical Systems.

Dr. Esser started his career with Daimler-Benz as a Staff Scientist and member of the Corporate Technology Exchange Program. He was responsible for innovative power conversion for rail applications.

Dr. Esser received his Diplom-Ing. (MS) and Dr.-Ing (PhD) in Electrical Engineering from the University of Aachen, Germany in 1988 and 1992 respectively. He published a book on wireless transmission of electric power and signals for robots, over 10 papers, received an Outstanding Prize Paper Award jointly from the IEEE/Industrial Application Society and the IEE Japan (1993), received a post Doc scholarship from the German Research Foundation (DfG), holds 12 US patents and received numerous company awards including GE’s Dushman award, GE’s highest ranking team award.
